Games + Me

I am a Computer Science and Creative Technology graduate with a strong interest in game development, technical systems, design, and interactive worldbuilding. Working in games is one of my main professional goals.

I am especially drawn to game development because it brings together systems thinking, design, atmosphere, player interaction, and technical problem solving. As a former CU Buffs football student-athlete, I also bring discipline, teamwork, and consistency into the way I build and collaborate.

Career Direction

I want to work in the game industry and keep growing in gameplay systems, technical design, multiplayer, UI, and creative development.

Engines + Tools

Godot, Unreal Engine, Blender, HTML/CSS/JavaScript, prototyping workflows, and creative tech tools that let me move between design and development.

How I Build

I like learning by making, solving technical problems, and building projects that combine creative direction with real implementation.

Welcome to Suburbia

Welcome to Suburbia is one of my main game projects and a strong example of the kind of work I want to keep doing. The game is built around a multiplayer suburban setting that slowly becomes more disturbing over time, using procedural neighborhoods, interactive systems, and a tonal mix of absurd humor and creeping horror.

Core idea

Players explore an infinite-feeling neighborhood where normal suburban life hides something demonic underneath. The concept is about taking a familiar place and making it increasingly wrong, strange, and hostile.

What I made

This project involves gameplay systems, multiplayer work, procedural world design, environmental tone, interaction design, and overall creative direction for the experience.

Why it matters

It reflects a lot of what I care about in game development: atmosphere, systems, player discovery, experimentation, technical problem solving, and building a world with a strong identity.

StealSteel

StealSteel is an in-progress multiplayer PvP project centered on a steal-and-score style objective loop. Players compete to grab the objective, move quickly, evade opponents, and successfully escape or score before being stopped.

Core idea

The game is built around movement, pressure, and player-versus-player interaction, with the objective creating a constant push between offense, defense, and escape.

What I am building

This project focuses on multiplayer implementation, gameplay systems, player movement, interaction logic, and creating a competitive objective loop that feels fast and readable.

Why it matters

It shows another side of my game work: tighter competitive systems, multiplayer logic, and designing around player behavior, pacing, and match flow.
